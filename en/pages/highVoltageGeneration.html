HIGH VOLTAGE GENERATION


High voltage generation is the process of producing lower electrical voltage. High voltage can be used for various purposes such as electrical power transmission, industrial equipment, and scientific research.


AC HIGH VOLTAGE GENERATION

To generate alternating high voltage in the laboratory, a three-winding high voltage test transformer is used (can be used for cascade circuits). This type of transformer has a very large turns ratio between the high voltage winding H and the low voltage or excitation winding E.


DC HIGH VOLTAGE GENERATION

DC high voltage for testing can be generated by rectifying the DC high voltage source from the test transformer. Commonly used semiconductor materials for this purpose are selenium, germanium, and silicon. Half wave rectifier circuit with voltage leveling capacitor.


IMPULSE HIGH VOLTAGE GENERATION

Impulse voltage waves are surge waves that rise quickly at the front of the wave and fall slowly at the back of the wave. Impulse voltage waves can be present in electrical and electronic equipment as dangerous overvoltages and need to be limited in size.
